Jewelry Care Guide

Keep your precious pieces sparkling and beautiful for years to come with these essential care tips.

1

Store Jewelry Properly

Store each piece separately in soft pouches or lined jewelry boxes to prevent scratches and tangling. Keep jewelry away from direct sunlight and humidity to maintain its luster and prevent tarnishing.

2

Clean Regularly

Use a soft, lint-free cloth to gently wipe your jewelry after each wear. For deeper cleaning, use warm water with mild soap and a soft brush. Dry thoroughly before storing to prevent water spots and damage.

3

Remove Before Activities

Take off your jewelry before swimming, exercising, showering, or doing household chores. Chemicals in pools, sweat, and cleaning products can damage metals and gemstones over time.

4

Avoid Chemicals

Keep jewelry away from perfumes, hairsprays, lotions, and cosmetics. Apply these products before putting on your jewelry. Chemicals can cause discoloration and weaken metal settings.

5

Professional Inspection

Have your jewelry professionally inspected and cleaned at least once a year. Jewelers can check for loose stones, worn prongs, and other issues that need repair before they become serious problems.

6

Handle With Care

Always handle jewelry gently and avoid pulling or tugging on delicate chains and settings. Put jewelry on last when getting dressed and remove it first when undressing to minimize contact with clothing.

7

Know Your Materials

Different materials require different care. Research the specific needs of your jewelry's metals and gemstones. Some stones are more delicate and require special handling and cleaning methods.

8

Insurance and Documentation

Keep receipts, certificates, and photographs of your valuable pieces. Consider insuring expensive jewelry and update appraisals regularly to ensure adequate coverage in case of loss or damage.
